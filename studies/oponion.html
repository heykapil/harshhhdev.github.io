Oponion

Creating, voting, & sharing polls made simple

Launch Project

The Idea

At school, we often do surveys & stuff in different classes. Generally, for this, we generally tend to learn towards/use TypeForm & Google Forms. Google Forms is decent, & I really love TypeForm for it's intuitive form experience. Although, I feel like a new user has to go through a longer process - and it isn't exactly the best for creating short & fun polls. Having too much time during Summer Break, I decided to take up the project.

Planning

As mentioned above, I wanted to keep make it so that the user doesn't have to go through a long process to create polls. That's why, when you open Oponion, you directly go to the poll creation form.

After that, everything is rather self-explanatory. Just type a title, and add/remove options. I added a handy chart visualisation tool at the bottom to help you visualise your poll's chart. My goal with this project was to keep everything to the point, & nothing more.

I decided I wasn't going to create a seperate backend for this project either - primarily because I believe this will just make things more complicated than they need to be. I used the handy Firebase Realtime Database, which integrated easily in my frontend.

Tech Stack

For this project, I decided to go with the handy Next.js & the amazing Stitches CSS-in-JS library to build the frontend.

As usual, I used TypeScript for it's added benefits over JavaScript. Once you get a habit of using TypeScript for projects, using JavaScript just feels weird.

For charts & visualisation, I was initially planning to use Chart.js due to it's widespread popularity amongst the JavaScript community. However, I found a gem called Apache ECharts, and I instantly fell in love with the customisation & the amazing look/feel.

As mentioned above, I wanted to try out something new for the database this time. This was the first time that I was building a "fullstack" application without a backend, so I decided to go with the Firebase Realtime Database, which easily integrated with my project & worked like a charm!

Deployment

For deploying this application, I used Vercel. I absolutely LOVE everything about Vercel, and since I built my application with Next.js](https://nextjs.org), Vercel was the obvious choice for deploying. I love how easy Vercel has made my life - free & automated deployments, amazing navigation & control, along with automated deployments with GitHub
